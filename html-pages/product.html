Product Overview

Gym Attendance Tracker

A crowd-sourced check-in tool built for busy University of Iowa students who want to plan smarter workouts.

The Problem

Students at the University of Iowa balance heavy course loads, jobs, and extracurriculars. When they carve out 45 minutes to work out, the last thing they can afford is arriving at an overcrowded gym. There is currently no way to know how busy a facility is before you walk through the door.

The Solution

Gym Attendance Tracker (GAT) is a simple, crowd-sourced check-in app. Students tap Check In when they arrive at a gym and Check Out when they leave. The live count is visible to anyone — no account required.

Over time, as more students use the app, the counts become increasingly accurate. GAT grows more useful with every check-in.

Who It Is For

The Busy Undergraduate

A frequent exerciser — four or more sessions per week — juggling a demanding schedule. They want confidence that they can complete their full workout routine without spending half of it waiting for equipment. GAT gives them the information they need to decide: go now, go later, or pick a different facility.

Key Features

  • Live check-in counts for CRWC, Field House, and Fitness East
  • One-tap check-in and check-out — no account or login required
  • Anonymous session tracking stored in the browser
  • Busyness indicator (Quiet / Moderate / Busy / Very Busy) based on current headcount
  • Counts auto-refresh every 30 seconds
  • Check-ins auto-expire after 3 hours so stale data never accumulates
  • Fully mobile-responsive for checking on the go

Success Metrics

Metric Type Goal
Students who complete their planned workout Lagging indicator Increase over baseline
Self-reported stress when entering the gym Leading indicator Decrease via survey
Gym visits where occupancy matched expectations Leading indicator Increase over baseline
Number of active check-ins per day Engagement Grow week over week

How the Data Works

GAT uses crowd-sourced data. There is no integration with the university's ID card scanner system — the counts represent students who have checked in through this app. Accuracy improves with adoption. Each check-in is anonymous and expires automatically after three hours.

The goal is to pitch the concept to University of Iowa Rec Services and eventually integrate with their existing infrastructure for real-time, institution-wide occupancy data.
